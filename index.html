Beth, you are a carrier for three diseases

Being a carrier is common and does not mean you have the disease.
Each child's risk of inheriting a disease is undetermined until we test your partner.

  • Cystic Fibrosis

    Characterized by impaired lung and digestive function due to thick, sticky mucus. The disease typically worsens over time and currently has no cure.

  • Usher Syndrome Type 1 (USH1)

    Presents with com­plete hear­ing loss from birth, prob­lems with bal­ance and pro­gres­sive loss of sight start­ing be­fore pu­ber­ty.

  • GJB2 Related hearing loss

    Hearing loss that varies from mild to profound, is typically present at birth, and does not worsen over time.

You are unlikely to be a carrier for the 197 other diseases tested.

To know your children's risk:

Request partner's test

Until we know if your partner is a carrier for the same diseases as you, the risk to your children is undetermined.

If your partner IS NOT a carrier:

Each child is unlikely to have the disease.

There is still less than a 1% chance you and your partner could be a carrier as we cannot test for all possible variants of the disease.

If your partner IS a carrier:

Each child has a 25% chance of having the disease and a 75% chance of not having the disease.

The odds of your partner being a carrier depend on the disease, family history, and ethnicity.

Why should my partner BE tested?

Some parents want to know their children's chance of inheriting a disease so that, if needed, they can:

  • Take precautionary measures during pregnancy.
  • Prepare for a child with disease before birth
  • Alter family plans.

Why should my partner NOT BE tested?

Some parents do not want to know if they are a carrier of a disease, or want to wait until birth to know if their child has inherited any disease.

Send your report to your family doctor

If you have siblings, they have 50% chance of being a carrier of the same diseases as you. Some siblings appreciate knowing this information, but some do not. Consider these reasons before telling family.
