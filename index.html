Your children's risk is still undetermined

Until we know if your partner is a carrier for the same diseases as you, the risk to your children is undetermined.

If your partner IS NOT a carrier:

Each child is unlikely to have the disease.

There is still less than a 1% chance you and your partner could be a carrier as we cannot test for all possible variants of the disease.

If your partner IS a carrier:

Each child has a 25% chance of having the disease and a 75% chance of not having the disease.

The odds of your partner being a carrier depend on the disease, family history, and ethnicity.

Why should my partner BE tested?

Some parents want to know their children's chance of inheriting a disease so that, if needed, they can:

  • Take precautionary measures during pregnancy.
  • Prepare for a child with disease before birth
  • Alter family plans.

Why should my partner NOT BE tested?

Some parents do not want to know if they are a carrier of a disease, or want to wait until birth to know if their child has inherited any disease.
